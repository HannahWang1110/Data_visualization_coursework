What behind the life expectancy?


This story is arose from an accident discovery about worldwide life expectancy and a guess that the difference in diet structure would be one factor making life expectancy in analogous countries distinct.

Life Expectancy

In the recent years, more and more people, not only in developed countries but also in developing coutries begin to pay much attention to their health and life expectancy. Based on this, I collect data about worldwide life expectancy and try to find some interesting points.


Life expectancy trend


The life expectancy data used here is collected from kaggle. The line graph shows the life expectancy is generally increasing in each country from 2005 to 2015. To make the trend clearer, I only select some sample countries to present. We can see that there are no big changes on the rank of life expectancy among these countries in 10 years, expecially after 2008. In addition, some records in specific countries are missing after 2011. Therefore, I will use life expectancy data in 2011 to represent data from a 10-year period.



Country(Upper&High Income)

The average income per capita of countries from different income groups are in different levels. For example, citizens in a country from 'High Income group' have higher average income than those in a country from 'Upper Income Group'. It is an indicator to a country's economy standard.


Country(Upper Income)


The map below records the value of life expectancy in sample countries from 'Upper Income Group' in 2011. From the map, we can find that the pattern of life length in analogous countries from two regions, Europe & Central Asia and Latin America & Caribbean, are both unusual. There is an obvious gap between countries' life length in the same region. It is surprising because these countries in the same region, from the same income group are not only close to each other in geographical location, but also similar in culture, wealth, habits and even food source. So this will bring some questions. For example, why does this situation happen and what are the factors behind it?





Countries in Europe & Central Asia such as Albania and Bulgaria are close to each other but distinct in life expectancy, with 77.24 and 74.16 respectively.

Country(High Income)


Similar circumstance also happens in the high income group. In Europe & Central Asia, countries like Italy, Germany, Spain are next to each other. In North America, Canada is even inserted between North America and South America. However, the life expectancy gaps within these countries' in each region still exist.




Food Supply Structure

Due to the situation from two maps above, I make a guess that perhapes the difference in eating preference or called "diet structure" imposes an effect on the life length.

Sample Countries

Based on the guess, I pick some sample countries from hige income group and upper income group respectively. And prepare to compare the diet structure in these countries with regard to the regions. Sample countries with the life length values are shown in the charts below.




For each chart, the life length can be approximately grouped into three stages. I will pick up countries from three stages seperately and compare their diet structure.

Structure Analysis


Diet preference or diet structure can be explained as the composition of food intake. The structure comparison of countries from upper income group in two regions are shown as below.




The countries in this small multiple pie chart are sorted by descending life expectancy. Here, 'Food supply(kcal/capita/day)' refers to the calorie of daily food intake per person, and 'Food suppy quantity(kg/capita/yr)' refers to quantity of food intake per person every year. According to the chart, in Latin America & Caribbean, Cuba has the highest life expectancy, whereas Brazil is with the minimum. In this region, it seems that countries with lower food supply(kcal/capita/day) in Animal Products tend to have a higher value in life. Besides, it seems that countries with a shorter life length generally have larger intake in the quantity of Alcoholic Beverages and small intake in Fruits&Vegetables. This conclusion conforms to the common sense. However, in Europe & Central Asia, the situation in Animal Products is converse! We can see Albania with the highest life length also consume the largest proportion of animal products. To gain more information, let's have a look at the high income group.




The conclusion is same with the one acquired in Latin America & Caribbean in upper income world. High life expectancy countries tend to have more quantity intake in Fruits&Vegetables and less in Alcoholic Beverages. It is quite clear and obvious. In contrast, in Animal Products, though the same conclusion as before is available to acquire, the difference in animal products between Canada and the U.S. is not significant. So what happened in the Animal Products? Why is it reverse in in Europe & Central Asia? Firstly, we need to figure out what is included in the Animal Products.
